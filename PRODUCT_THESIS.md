# Product Thesis

## What Mirrors Is

Mirrors is creator intelligence infrastructure for businesses. We help agencies, brands, and platforms answer questions that raw follower counts and engagement rates cannot:

- Is this creator's audience real and relevant?
- Is growth organic or inflated?
- How does this creator compare to peers in the same niche?
- What changed over time, and why?

The product is not a dashboard of vanity metrics. It is an **interpretation layer** built on stored snapshots, versioned scoring, and transparent breakdowns.

## Who We Serve

| Segment | Role | What they need |
|---------|------|----------------|
| **Businesses (primary)** | Agencies, brands, talent managers | Reliable creator evaluation, exports, team workflows |
| **Consumers (funnel)** | Creators checking their own profile | Free or low-friction entry point; upsell to business tools |

Businesses pay. Consumers discover. The mobile app is one surface—not the product.

## The Moat

Data collection is commoditized. Platform APIs change, scrapers break, and competitors can copy ingestion.

Mirrors' defensibility is **interpretation**:

- Versioned, explainable scoring models
- Historical snapshots that enable trend analysis
- Consistent cross-creator comparison within a niche
- Breakdowns that a human can audit and trust

We win by being the system businesses cite when they say *"this is how we evaluate creators."*

## Design Principles

1. **Transparency** — Every score decomposes into visible inputs and weights. No black boxes.
2. **Snapshot-first** — Core product works offline from stored data. Live API calls are ingestion only.
3. **Simplicity** — Ship the smallest system that answers a real question. Add complexity only when validated.
4. **Modularity** — Platforms are plug-in connectors. Scoring is versioned and swappable.
5. **Consent & ethics** — We analyze public creator profiles for business evaluation. We do not surveil, profile without consent, or build credit/civic scores.

## What Success Looks Like

**Phase 1:** A business can connect or look up an Instagram creator, see a versioned score with a plain-language breakdown, and review historical snapshots in a web dashboard.

**Phase 2:** An agency searches a catalog of creators, compares scores, exports reports, and pays for the product.

**Phase 3+:** Multi-platform intelligence, recommendations, and eventually network-level influence mapping—always explainable, always snapshot-backed.

## What We Are Not Building

- A consumer social app
- A real-time analytics firehose tied to platform API uptime
- Opaque "trust scores" with hidden signals
- Surveillance, facial recognition, or civic/credit scoring
